The market at the time

Few games translated to French, except:

We studied what the market was offering: lots of games in english, often poorly translated from another language. Rarely in French. The quality varied a lot, most games looking fan-made. Some had great main characters, like Is it Love, but usually they had really poor backgrounds and worst UIs.

Some numbers

Is it Love stats in 2016

Despite the poor production value, those games were selling. Thanks to great cast of characters and themes, and ease of use. We thought: we can do even better than this.

The Concept

  • Mainly female audience
  • Hot BoyZ!!
  • Paris
  • Modern Day, relatable environment
  • Phone mode
  • Pretty graphics
  • "Straight to the point" story
So we started discussing what we wanted for the game. From our competitors we kept: a female audience target, the cast of cute boys surrounded by likable friends. But we wanted to add a family aspect, give the whole thing a "village feeling" (Plus belle la vie). We also wanted to propose a different theme than the over used business office romance, school drama and heroic-fantasy romance. And we settled for: first job at a bakery in a charming neightboorhood. We also wanted to paint a beautiful Paris. The one people see in movies, not the actual one of course ðŸ˜œ
